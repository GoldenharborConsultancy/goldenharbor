How to Check Your CTOS Score in Malaysia

Updated: May 2026 · 5 min read

Your CTOS score is one of the first things banks look at when you apply for a loan. If your score is low, many banks will reject your application before even looking at your income.

In this guide, We'll show you exactly how to check your CTOS score — for free and paid — and explain what the numbers mean for your loan approval chances.

What is a CTOS Score?

CTOS is Malaysia's leading credit reporting agency. Your CTOS score ranges from 300 to 850 and is calculated based on your credit history, payment behaviour, and existing debt commitments.

This is separate from CCRIS — CCRIS is a centralised database from Bank Negara that records all your loan and credit card payments. Banks check both.

Method 1: Free CTOS Check (Basic)

CTOS offers a free credit report once every 12 months under the Credit Reporting Agencies Act. Here's how:

  1. Visit ctos.com.my and click "Get My Credit Report"
  2. Register with your IC number, name, and email
  3. Verify your identity — you may need to upload a photo of your IC
  4. Download your free report — it includes your basic credit score and summary
💡 Tip: The free report gives you a general picture. For detailed information including individual account records, consider the paid version (RM 15–30).

Method 2: CTOS Mobile App (RM 15)

Download the CTOS Mobile app from Google Play or Apple App Store.

  1. Register and verify your IC number
  2. Pay a one-time fee of RM 15
  3. Instantly view your full credit report including score, CCRIS records, and account history

This is the most convenient method and gives you the full picture including any red flags that banks will see.

How to Improve Your CTOS Score

If your score is lower than expected, don't panic. Here are practical ways to improve it:

  • Pay all bills on time — even a single late payment stays on your record for 12 months
  • Reduce credit card usage — try to keep utilisation below 30% of your limit
  • Don't apply for too many loans at once — each application creates an inquiry on your record
  • Clear small debts — settle any outstanding balances under RM 1,000 first
  • Avoid closing old credit cards — longer credit history helps your score

Common Questions

How often should We check my CTOS score?

At least once a year. If you're planning to apply for a loan, check 3–6 months beforehand so you have time to fix any issues.

Does checking my own score affect it?

No. Checking your own score is a "soft inquiry" and does not affect your rating. Only loan applications trigger "hard inquiries."

Can We check someone else's CTOS?

No — you can only check your own unless you have written consent. For joint loan applications, both applicants need to check separately.
