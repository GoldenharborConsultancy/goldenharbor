Documents Needed for Home Loan in Malaysia

Updated: May 2026 · 5 min read

Applying for a home loan? The #1 reason applications get delayed or rejected is incomplete or incorrect documents.

This checklist covers everything you need — whether you're salaried, self-employed, or a business owner. Print it or save it, and tick off each item before submission.

📋 Complete Home Loan Document Checklist

A. Basic Personal Documents
  • MyKad (front & back) — yours and any joint borrowers Must be clear, not expired
  • Birth certificate (if applying for young borrower / first home scheme) For certain schemes only
  • Marriage certificate (if joint application with spouse) Certified copy from JPN
B. Income Documents — Salaried Employees
  • Latest 3–6 months payslips Must show basic salary, allowances, deductions
  • EPF statement (latest 6–12 months) Download from We-Akaun (KWSP)
  • Bank statements (latest 3–6 months) Shows salary crediting and spending pattern
  • Employment confirmation letter Signed by employer, stamped if possible
  • EA Form or tax assessment for last 2 years For high loan amounts > RM 500k
C. Income Documents — Self-Employed / Business Owners
  • Form B / Income Tax Return (latest 2 years) Must show declared income
  • Tax payment receipts & Sijil PendaftaraanMajikan Proof of tax compliance
  • Bank statements (latest 6–12 months) Business & personal accounts
  • SSM registration certificate Valid & up to date
  • Business profile from SSM Shows business address, partners, nature of business
  • Latest 6 months of EPF contributions (if any) Even voluntary contributions help
  • Audited / management accounts (latest 2 years) For larger loan amounts
D. Property Documents
  • Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA) Signed & stamped — the main document
  • Copy of property title (Geran / Master Title) From developer or current owner
  • Valuation report (for subsale / second-hand properties) Required by most banks for subsale
  • Booking form / letter of offer from developer For new projects
E. Additional Documents (If Applicable)
  • Letter of explanation (if you have past CCRIS issues) Be honest — explain what happened and what changed
  • Rental income documents (if using rental as additional income) Tenancy agreement + bank statements showing rent credited
  • Supporting documents for commission / bonus / overtime Letter from employer + 6 months proof
  • Joint borrower's complete documents Same set of docs as main applicant
💡 Pro Tip: Most banks allow you to submit digital copies (scans or photos) for initial assessment. Keep the originals ready for verification when the bank processes your application.

Common Mistakes That Delay Your Home Loan

  • Blurry copies — scan at 300 DPI minimum, no shadowed or cut-off documents
  • Wrong bank statements — some banks want 3 months, some want 6. Check first
  • Missing pages — make sure every page of your bank statement is included
  • Old EPF statements — must be within the last 3 months (not last year's)
  • Inconsistent signatures — your signature on the application form must match your IC

Which Bank to Choose?

Different banks have different policies for:

  • Minimum income threshold — some banks require RM 3,000/month, others RM 5,000
  • Maximum DSR — Maybank typically allows up to 60%, while CIMB can be stricter
  • Interest rates — BLR, BR, and effective rates vary weekly
  • Margin of financing — some banks offer 90% loan, others only 80% for subsale properties

Frequently Asked Questions

Can We apply for a home loan online?

Yes — most Malaysian banks now accept online applications (Maybank2u, CIMB Clicks, etc.). However, you'll still need to submit physical or digital copies of documents.

How long does home loan approval take?

Typically 3–14 working days. If all documents are complete, some banks can approve within 48 hours for salaried employees. Self-employed applications take longer (up to 2–3 weeks).

Can We apply with multiple banks at once?

Yes, but be careful. Each application triggers a CCRIS inquiry. Too many in a short time looks desperate. We recommend applying to no more than 2–3 banks at a time.
