Documents Needed for Personal & SME Loan in Malaysia

Updated: May 2026 · 5 min read

The #1 reason personal and SME loan applications get delayed or rejected is incomplete or incorrect documents.

This checklist covers everything you need — whether you're applying for a personal loan or an SME/business loan. Print it or save it, and tick off each item before submission.

📋 Complete Document Checklist

A. Basic Personal Documents
  • MyKad (front & back) — yours and any joint borrowers Must be clear, not expired
  • Birth certificate (if applying for young borrower schemes) For certain schemes only
  • Marriage certificate (if joint application with spouse) Certified copy from JPN
B. Income Documents — Salaried Employees (Personal Loan)
  • Latest 3–6 months payslips Must show basic salary, allowances, deductions
  • EPF statement (latest 6–12 months) Download from i-Akaun (KWSP)
  • Bank statements (latest 3–6 months) Shows salary crediting and spending pattern
  • Employment confirmation letter Signed by employer, stamped if possible
  • EA Form or tax assessment for last 2 years For higher loan amounts
C. Income Documents — Self-Employed / Business Owners (Personal & SME Loan)
  • Form B / Income Tax Return (latest 2 years) Must show declared income
  • Tax payment receipts & Sijil Pendaftaran Proof of tax compliance
  • Bank statements (latest 6–12 months) Business & personal accounts
  • SSM registration certificate Valid & up to date
  • Business profile from SSM Shows business address, partners, nature of business
  • Latest 6 months of EPF contributions (if any) Even voluntary contributions help
  • Audited / management accounts (latest 2 years) For larger SME loan amounts
D. SME / Business Loan — Additional Documents
  • Business loan proposal / purpose letter Explain what the loan is for (working capital, expansion, equipment, etc.)
  • List of trade debtors & creditors For existing businesses
  • Supplier / customer contracts (if any) Shows business sustainability
  • Business bank statements (latest 12 months) Separate from personal accounts
  • Company stamp / chop For official application forms
E. Additional Documents (If Applicable)
  • Letter of explanation (if you have past CCRIS issues) Be honest — explain what happened and what changed
  • Rental income documents (if using rental as additional income) Tenancy agreement + bank statements
  • Supporting documents for commission / bonus / overtime Letter from employer + 6 months proof
  • Joint borrower's complete documents Same set of docs as main applicant
💡 Pro Tip: Most banks allow you to submit digital copies (scans or photos) for initial assessment. Keep the originals ready for verification when the bank processes your application.

Common Mistakes That Delay Your Loan

  • Blurry copies — scan at 300 DPI minimum, no shadowed or cut-off documents
  • Wrong bank statements — some banks want 3 months, some want 6. Check first
  • Missing pages — make sure every page of your bank statement is included
  • Old EPF statements — must be within the last 3 months (not last year's)
  • Inconsistent signatures — your signature on the application form must match your IC
  • SSM not updated — for SME loans, an expired SSM registration will be rejected immediately

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply for a personal loan online?

Yes — most Malaysian banks now accept online applications (Maybank2u, CIMB Clicks, etc.). However, you'll still need to submit digital copies of documents.

How long does loan approval take?

Personal loans: typically 1–7 working days. SME loans: 1–4 weeks depending on the amount and complexity.

Can I apply with multiple banks at once?

Yes, but be careful. Each application triggers a CCRIS inquiry. Too many in a short time looks desperate. We recommend applying to no more than 2–3 banks at a time — we can advise which ones are best for your profile.
