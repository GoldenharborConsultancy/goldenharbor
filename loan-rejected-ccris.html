Loan Rejected Because of CCRIS? Here's How to Fix It

Updated: May 2026 · 6 min read

You applied for a loan. The bank said no. When you asked why, they mentioned "CCRIS issues."

You're not alone — this is the #1 reason bank loans get rejected in Malaysia. The good news? Most CCRIS issues can be fixed.

What is CCRIS?

CCRIS stands for Credit Reporting and Reference Information System, managed by Bank Negara Malaysia (the central bank). It records every Malaysian's credit activity:

  • Loan accounts — housing, car, personal, business
  • Credit cards — limits, balances, payment history
  • Payment patterns — on time or late, and by how many days

Banks always check your CCRIS before approving any loan. It's mandatory.

Step-by-Step: How to Fix Your CCRIS

Step 1: Get Your CCRIS Report

You can get your CCRIS report for RM 2 at any Bank Negara Malaysia office (Kuala Lumpur, Penang, Johor, etc.) or via their online portal at eccris.bnm.gov.my.

💡 Tip: The RM 2 CCRIS report is different from your CTOS report. Get both — they show different things. Contact us if you need help reading them.

Step 2: Identify the Problem Accounts

Look for accounts marked with 2, 3, or more on the CCRIS grid. These are the accounts causing the rejection. Common problems:

  • A credit card you forgot you had with RM 50 outstanding
  • A car loan where you paid late 2–3 times last year
  • A PTPTN loan in arrears
  • Too many personal loans running at the same time

Step 3: Settle Arrears Immediately

If you have overdue payments, pay them now. Even partial payment helps. Call the bank or lender and ask for a settlement amount. Keep proof of payment.

⚠️ Important: Paying overdue amounts doesn't instantly remove the record. CCRIS shows your last 12 months of payment patterns. If you pay on time consistently for 6–12 months, the record gradually clears.

Step 4: Reduce Your Debt Burden (DSR)

Your Debt Service Ratio (DSR) is your total monthly commitments divided by your income. Most banks require:

  • Below 50% — excellent chance
  • 50–60% — borderline, depends on bank
  • Above 60% — almost always rejected

To lower your DSR: pay off small loans, consolidate debts, or settle credit card balances.

Step 5: Wait Before Reapplying

Don't apply to multiple banks at once. Each application is recorded on CCRIS and too many in a short period looks desperate.

Wait 3–6 months of clean payment history before reapplying.

Common Questions

Can We remove CCRIS records?

No — the records are factual (your actual payment history). They automatically age off after 12 months of good behaviour. There is no "CCRIS cleanup service" that removes records.

⚠️ Scam Alert: Be very careful of anyone who promises to "remove" or "clean" your CCRIS records for a fee. This is a common scam. Only time and consistent payment can fix CCRIS.

Can We get a loan if We have bad CCRIS?

Some banks have slightly more relaxed policies, but you'll pay higher interest rates. It's better to fix your CCRIS first and get better rates.

How long does bad CCRIS last?

Negative payment records stay on CCRIS for 12 months from the date of the late payment. Older records eventually drop off.
